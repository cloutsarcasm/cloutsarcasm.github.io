कलम की कहानी - A Heart's Silent Poetry

Do you remember your first love? That pure, innocent feeling that made your heart skip a beat? Mine began in those simple school corridors, where time seemed to slow down every morning as she walked past. Her dupatta would dance with the morning breeze, carrying with it a fragrance that made my heart write poetry I didn't even know I was capable of. She carried her books close to her heart - just as I carried her memories in mine.

"वो जब भी गुज़रती थी सुबह की धूप में,
खुशबुओं के मौसम जैसे छा जाते थे,
मैं कुछ लिखना चाहता था उस पल में,
पर लफ्ज़ भी शर्मा जाते थे..."

Our homes were just streets apart in the same mohalla, yet my heart felt the distance of oceans. Every evening, I would wait by my window, pretending to study, just to catch a glimpse of her walking home. Sometimes alone, sometimes with friends - but always carrying that smile that could light up the darkest corners of my heart. Those moments became the ink for pages I filled in secret:

"हर शाम उसी राह पे नज़रें टिकी रहतीं,
वो गुज़रे तो दिल की धड़कनें थमी रहतीं,
कुछ कहना चाहता था मैं हर रोज़ उससे,
पर बेज़ुबान आँखें बस नमी लिए रहतीं..."

Time is a strange painter - it colors our memories in hues we never expected. I watched her transform from a shy schoolgirl into a confident young woman, while I remained the silent admirer, collecting moments like precious stones. Then came that day - the day I saw her eyes sparkle for someone else. You know that moment when your heart breaks but somehow feels lighter? That's when I learned the true meaning of love.

"देखा जो उसे किसी और की बाहों में,
टूटा तो दिल, मगर मुस्कुराया भी,
उसकी खुशी में अपनी खुशी ढूंढ ली,
शायद प्यार का मतलब भी यही पाया मैंने..."

These pages of my diary became more than just a collection of words - they became the story of every heart that has loved silently, every soul that has found beauty in pain, every dreamer who learned that sometimes loving from afar is the purest form of love. Each verse I write is a celebration of that first love that taught me how to love without expecting anything in return.

Many ask me why my shayari touches hearts so deeply. Perhaps it's because these words aren't just poetry - they're echoes of real heartbeats, whispers of unspoken feelings, and stories of love that found its expression not in union, but in art. After all, isn't that what makes teenage love so beautiful? The purity of feelings that remain unchanged by time, unspoiled by expression, and immortalized in poetry.

"कुछ कहा नहीं, कुछ सुना नहीं,
फिर भी हर लफ्ज़ में तेरा किस्सा है,
तू मेरी नहीं, ये जानता हूं मैं,
पर तेरी यादों से मेरी दुनिया बसी है..."

Today, when I write these shayaris, I'm not just writing about my story - I'm writing about every heart that has ever loved purely, every soul that has found strength in silence, and every person who has learned that sometimes the most beautiful love stories are the ones that remain untold. Because in the end, some feelings are too pure to be expressed in words, yet too beautiful to remain unwritten.

"ये किस्सा मेरा नहीं, हर उस दिल की कहानी है,
जिसने चुपके से किसी को चाहा है,
हर वो लफ्ज़ जो कहा नहीं गया,
आज मेरी शायरी बन जाता है..."
