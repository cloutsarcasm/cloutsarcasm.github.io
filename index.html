It started in the corridors of our school, where every morning felt like a new page waiting to be written. She would pass by, her dupatta dancing with the wind, carrying books close to her heart. I didn't know then that those fleeting moments would become the ink of my shayari years later.

We lived in the same mohalla, and everyday I would see her walking home, sometimes alone, sometimes with friends. The distance between us was merely a few streets, but in my heart, it felt like an ocean of unspoken words. That's when the first words began forming in my diary:

"Wo guzarti hai jab bhi gali se,
Khushbuon ka karwan lag jaata hai,
Main likhta hoon, mitaata hoon,
Par har lafz mein uska naam aa jaata hai..."

Years passed like pages turning in a book. I saw her grow from a shy schoolgirl into a confident young woman. My feelings grew too, silent but deep, like roots of a tree searching for water. Then one day, I saw her smile differently at someone else. Her eyes held a sparkle I had always wished would be for me.

But love isn't always about possession; sometimes it's about pure appreciation of beauty, of existence. That realization became my inspiration:

"Tum khush ho, yahi kaafi hai,
Tumhari khushi mein meri duniya hai,
Main chahun bhi to kya kahun tumse,
Meri har nazm tumhari kahani hai..."

Today, these shayaris are not just words of unrequited love; they're celebrations of pure emotion. Every verse I write carries fragments of those school days, those street corners where our paths would cross, those moments when silence spoke louder than words. Some people write from imagination, but I write from a heart that learned to love without expecting anything in return.

My diary became my confidant, and these shayaris became my way of loving from afar. Perhaps that's why they resonate with so many - because true love isn't always about happy endings; sometimes it's about the beauty of feeling itself, about finding poetry in pain, and about transforming longing into art.

"Maine likha hai har lafz tumhare liye,
Par tumhe kabhi sunaya nahi,
Shayad yahi ishq ki khubsurati hai,
Ke maine tumhe kabhi bataya nahi..."
